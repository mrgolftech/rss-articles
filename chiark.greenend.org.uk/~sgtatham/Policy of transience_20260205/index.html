    • Usable by other people. I havenГўВҖВҷt talked about this aspect much, because most of my examples above have been about my personal workflow.     Other related practices¶

    If youГўВҖВҷre in an organisation which subjects you to this policy, what do you do? Your email archive probably has actually useful information in it, and if you have to delete it after a year, youГўВҖВҷll lose stuff you wanted to keep.

    (I donГўВҖВҷt think this violates the spirit of the records-management doctrine from the companyГўВҖВҷs point of view.     In a corporate environment, another thing youГўВҖВҷre probably concerned about (or should be) is reducing ГўВҖВҳbus factorГўВҖВҷ: there shouldnГўВҖВҷt be any important task that only one person can do, or important information that only one person knows.     I donГўВҖВҷt adopt this practice for my personal email, though! ItГўВҖВҷs one thing to have it imposed on you by an employer and look for the silver lining, but I wouldnГўВҖВҷt do it on my own initiative.

    Another kind of data thatГўВҖВҷs not well organised and lasts a long time is the configuration of a specific computer.     (IГўВҖВҷm mostly talking about systemwide configuration here.     IГўВҖВҷm vaguely aware that there are methods of managing physical computer systems via configuration files of some kind. Words like ГўВҖВҳAnsibleГўВҖВҷ and ГўВҖВҳPuppetГўВҖВҷ spring to mind.     Exceptions to the policy¶

    I donГўВҖВҷt apply this policy of transience to all parts of my life, or even all parts of my computer use.
